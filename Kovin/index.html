The Lives of Hongkong People During Japanese Occupation

On the Christmas day of 1941, following the defeat of the Battle of Hong Kong, the British Governor of Hong Kong surrendered to the Japanese, signifying the start of Japanese occupation in Hong Kong, a period, commonly referred to as “The Three Years and Eight Months”.

Looting by Japanese Troops

“...and many a sturdy private marched proudly about the streets of Hong Kong in the first days of the occupation with his left arm covered from wrist to elbow with one watch after another. The process of collecting these was the simple one of sticking a gun or a bayonet into anyone they could find, in or out of doors, and pointing to their victim's left wrist to indicate what they wanted.”
